## 5. Opposition Messaging & Counter-Arguments

### "Migration would be too expensive and disruptive."
**Counter:** We're already paying $30â€“70M/year â€” forever, with annual increases. Migration is a one-time investment that eliminates a permanent dependency. Schleswig-Holstein is migrating 25,000 workers. NYC has more resources than a German state. The real cost is staying.

**Counter:** France disagrees. Their government banned Google Workspace. Nextcloud and LibreOffice serve millions of users globally, including European governments with higher security requirements than NYC. "Best product" is a marketing claim, not a procurement standard.

**Counter:** No one is proposing pulling the plug tomorrow. We're proposing the city stop auto-renewing and begin a phased transition. The disruption argument is a stalling tactic â€” the same one used to block every infrastructure transition in history.

### "The city doesn't have the technical capacity to self-host."

### "Google's government contracts are separate from its education products."
**Counter:** They're the same company. The same engineering talent. The same cloud infrastructure. The same leadership that created Google Public Sector LLC and fired workers who protested Project Nimbus. You can't separate the balance sheet from the ethics sheet.

### "This is just anti-tech activism."
